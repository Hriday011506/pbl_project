AI-Driven Predictive Inventory & Dynamic Pricing System

Hypothesis / Project Statement:
Can artificial intelligence models leverage hyperlocal external signals such as weather forecasts, regional events, social media trends, and historical sales data to accurately predict SKU-level demand and autonomously optimize pricing and inventory decisions in real time to maximize retail profitability and minimize stock inefficiencies?

Problem Statement

Large retail chains manage thousands of SKUs across multiple locations. Traditional ERP and POS systems rely primarily on historical sales data and fixed reorder rules. These systems do not account for sudden hyperlocal demand fluctuations caused by weather changes, festivals, local events, breaking news, or viral social media trends.

As a result, retailers frequently face:

  • Stockouts during unexpected demand spikes
  • Overstock during demand drops
  • Revenue loss due to poor pricing strategies
  • High holding costs and product wastage

Therefore, there is a strong need for an intelligent system capable of predictive demand sensing and autonomous pricing adjustments.

Literature Review

Existing research highlights the role of AI and Machine Learning in retail demand forecasting. Studies from industry leaders such as BCG, Grid Dynamics, and Xoriant demonstrate that predictive analytics improves demand forecasting accuracy when incorporating weather data, local event signals, and consumer behavior analytics.

Dynamic pricing models using demand elasticity estimation have also been shown to increase revenue and optimize profit margins. However, many current implementations operate independently, focusing either on forecasting or pricing rather than an integrated system.

Research Gap

While several AI-based forecasting tools exist, most systems:

  • Do not integrate hyperlocal external signals in real time
  • Lack autonomous dynamic pricing mechanisms
  • Do not simulate financial impact across different scenarios
  • Operate as advisory systems rather than automated decision engines

This project aims to bridge this gap by creating a unified AI-driven predictive inventory and dynamic pricing framework.
